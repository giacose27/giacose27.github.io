Plately

Branding & Packaging

Project Overview

The goal of this project was to create branding and packaging for a fictional meal subscription service called Plately. They were looking for a brand identity that is fresh, modern, reliable and flavorful. Their mission is to bring fresh ingredients to your door to keep meal prep stress-free.

Research and Specifications

I began this project by researching various meal subscription services. I began thinking about the colors and font styles I would be using.

First Draft

From there, I designed the logo variations and made a quick mockup of the logo. During this stage I also began searching for mockup templates and downloading the ones I would be using. I like to have at least four mockups for each project.

Work in Progress

Once I had my logos and mockup templates, I started creating a pattern that would be used on the boxes and other items. I wanted to keep the patten simple, so I just used the "P" from the logo. I made both black and white versions of the pattern to match the logo variants.

Obstacles and Solutions

The most difficult part of this project was designing the brand to feel fresh and different without straying from the prompt. This was especially tricky with the color palette. I was able to overcome this problem by using a lot of white in the design to offset the bright and dark colors in the palette.

Critique and Revisions

When I asked for critique on this project, it was recommended that I make the words in the menu subheaders detached and fluid, with an egg yolk background. I implemented those ideas and made alternating egg yolk and maple syrup styles.

Next Steps

To finalize this branding, I created mockups of all the brand assets I created and designed an Instagram post.

Final Product and Mockups

The final product is a fun, modern brand that includes various logo styles, coffee cup designs, a large menu and mini cafe tabletop menu! The logo designs have many uses, with just one example being a paper holder for a coffee cup.
