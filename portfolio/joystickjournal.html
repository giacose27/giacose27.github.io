Joystick Journal

Magazine

Project Overview

The goal of this project was to design a conceptual gaming magazine called Joystick Journal for F.I.R.S.T. Institute. The key achievements of this project were fitting all of the text and images in as well as fitting the information in the table. These were also the major challenges of the project. Another major challenge was Adobe removing the fonts I was using halfway through the project.

Research and Specifications

This project was created in InDesign using 8 facing pages. I began by researching magazines overall and then narrowing down my search to gaming magazines. I began by making 4 sketches and picking the best spreads to move forward with. I then found the images using Unsplash and built up my color palette from the cover image.

First Draft

The first draft of this project was very simple. I began by bringing in the magazine text and ad I made in the 103 course. I began noticing where images needed to be moved and text needed to be adjusted in order to fill the entire magazine. This is also the stage where I chose the fonts and came up with a general layout. This is the part of the project where I created the parent pages and set up the folio, byline, and running headers. I also, set up my character and paragraph styles.

Work in Progress

I continued to add in images and shift text to make sure the magazine was organized, easy to read, and well designed. This is where I encountered my first challenge: spacing all of the text out to fill out the entire magazine. I decided the best thing to do was to find more images to add.

Obstacles and Solutions

There were two major challenges during this project. The first was choosing new fonts for the magazine halfway through the project after Adobe removed the original ones I chose. Luckly, I frequently search for new Google and Adobe fonts that I think could be used in future projects, so I always have plenty on my computer. This saved me a lot of time searching for new fonts to download. I was able to quickly update the paragraph and character styles and apply the new fonts to the entire magazine.


The second challenge was fitting the data into the table in a way that is well designed and easy to read. I overcame this by changing the font sizes, alignment, and individually adjust the tracking and leading for each data point and header.

Critique and Revisions

The critique I received pointed out that my article parts, the kickers, pull-quotes, etc., were missing. For my revisions, I quickly added those style elements in and saved them as their own paragraph styles.

Next Steps

To finalize this magazine, I created a print version and a web interactive version. In includes 2 articles, a data table and 14 images.

Final Product and Mockups

The final product is an 8 page magazine with both a print and web interactive version.
