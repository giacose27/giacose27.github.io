Skate Shop

UX/UI Conceptual Website & Logo

Project Overview

The goal of this project was to wireframe and design a website for a fictional skate shop. This project was made for F.I.R.S.T. Institute.

Research and Specifications

I knew that I wanted to give this website a Y2K vibe. So, I began by researching the Y2K aesthetic and making a moodboard in FigJam.

First Draft

From there, I brought my moodboard and color palette into Figma and began creating the frames and wireframes. During this stage, I also created the logo.

Work in Progress

I started looking for the mockups that I would use for the coffee cups and menus. During this process I found more ideas for brand assets I could create.

Obstacles and Solutions

Choosing the typograhy for Daily Yolk was a major obstacle during this project. Once I chose the font for the logo, pairing it with a contrasting subheader font became tricky. I searched through my fonts and eventually decided to go with Nitti Typewriter. I think this font fits the vibe of the brand perfectly.

Critique and Revisions

When I asked for critique on this project, it was recommended that I make the words in the menu subheaders detatched and fluid, with a egg yolk background. I implemented those ideas and made alternating egg yolk and maple syrup styles.

Next Steps

To finalize this branding, I created mockups of all the brand assets I created and designed and Instagram post.

Final Product and Mockups

The final product is a fun, modern brand that includes various logo styles, coffee cup designs, a large menu and mini cafe tabletop menu! The logo designs have many uses, with just one example being a paper holder for the coffee cups.
